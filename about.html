HISTORY

Kwekwe Polytechnic began life in the late 1970s when Union Carbide (Pvt) Ltd – later renamed ZIMASCO – built a training institute on a former farm owned by Mr Judd, to develop skilled workers for the mining and heavy‑industry sector. Construction finished in 1980, just as Zimbabwe won independence, and the following year the newly equipped campus was donated to the government and people of Zimbabwe. The first intake of students arrived in January 1981, studying Automotive, Electrical and Mechanical Engineering, and the official opening took place on 4 December 1981, presided over by Deputy Prime Minister Cde. S.V Muzenda, with Eng. E.C Judd serving as the inaugural principal 

Over the next two decades the institution expanded its portfolio: the Commerce Studies Division launched in 1984, the Adult and Continuing Education Division followed, and the NASS Division was added in 2003 – the same year the technical college was formally upgraded to a polytechnic. In August 2004 the college received a mandate to award degrees, beginning with a Bachelor of Technology (Honours) in Industrial and Manufacturing Engineering in February 2005, later joined by a B.Tech (Honours) in Electrical Power and Manufacturing Engineering in 2011. Today Kwekwe Polytechnic continues to grow as a leading, degree‑granting institution, offering a range of certificates, diplomas and honours degrees across engineering, commerce, applied sciences and continuing education

MISSION

Kwekwe Polytechnic's mission is generally aligned with objectives common to polytechnic institutions in Zimbabwe and globally, focusing on:

Typical Focus Area

  1. Technical and Vocational Training:
    Offering hands‑on, competency‑based education in fields like engineering, business, and applied sciences.
  2. Industry Relevance:
    Ensuring curricula match current industry standards and market demands.
  3. Community Development:
    Extending services and training to local communities, fostering entrepreneurship and skills transfer.
  4. Research and Innovation:
    Encouraging applied research and innovation to solve real‑world problems.

VISION

Kwekwe Polytechnic envisions becoming a leading centre of excellence in technical, vocational and higher‑education training, recognised regionally for producing highly competent, innovative professionals who drive sustainable industrial and socio‑economic development.

It aspires to be a catalyst for Zimbabwe’s transformation by delivering world‑class, industry‑relevant curricula, fostering research and entrepreneurship, and nurturing graduates who embody integrity, creativity and a lifelong‑learning mindset.
